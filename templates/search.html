Evidence for wavelike energy transfer through quantum coherence in photosynthetic systems

G.S. Engel, T.R. Calhoun, E.L. Read, et al.·Nature·2007

Photosynthetic complexes are exquisitely tuned to capture solar light efficiently, then transmit the excitation energy to reaction centres, where long term energy storage is initiated. The energy transfer mechanism is often described by semiclassical models that invoke 'hopping' of excited-state populations along discrete energy levels. Two-dimensional electronic spectroscopy of the Fenna-Matthews-Olson (FMO) bacteriochlorophyll complex reveals direct evidence for remarkably long-lived wavelike electronic quantum coherence playing an important part in energy transfer processes...

Quantum biology: Introduction

P. Ball·Nature Physics·2011

The idea that quantum mechanics might play a fundamental role in biology is not new. But recent experiments on light-harvesting complexes have provided the first direct evidence for quantum coherence in biological systems at physiological temperatures. Coherent Intradimer Dynamics and Contradictory Interpretations of Two-Dimensional Electronic Spectra of Photosynthetic Light-Harvesting Complexes

D. Hayes, G.B. Griffin, G.S. Engel·Science·2013

The observation of long-lived coherent oscillations in two-dimensional electronic spectra of photosynthetic complexes has sparked intense debate regarding their physical origin and functional significance. We present a detailed theoretical analysis of intradimer dynamics, demonstrating that vibronic coupling plays a crucial role in sustaining these coherences. Our results suggest that the standard purely electronic interpretation must be revised to account for the complex interplay between electronic and vibrational degrees of freedom...
