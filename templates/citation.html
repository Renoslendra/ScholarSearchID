Query: Implications of CRIPSR-Cas9 off-target mutations in somatic cell therapies

The application of CRISPR-Cas9 in somatic cell therapies presents significant potential for treating genetic disorders, but off-target mutations remain a primary safety concern. Recent comprehensive genomic analyses indicate that while high-fidelity Cas9 variants have reduced off-target cleavage rates by up to 85% [1], undetectable macro-deletions and complex genomic rearrangements can still occur at target sites [2].


These unintentional genomic alterations pose theoretical risks of oncogenesis, particularly when targeting highly proliferative stem cell populations ex vivo prior to engraftment [3]. To mitigate these risks, regulatory frameworks increasingly mandate orthogonal validation methods, combining in silico predictive modeling with empirical whole-genome sequencing workflows (e.g., GUIDE-seq, DISCOVER-seq) to rigorously profile the off-target landscape before clinical translation [4].
